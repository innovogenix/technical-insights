# Custom Fields in IFS Cloud: Extend Your ERP Without Compromising It

Every business is unique — but out-of-the-box ERP systems don't always know that. You might be tracking a supplier certification number, a project-specific category, or a customer preference that standard IFS screens simply don't have a home for. That's exactly where Custom Fields come in.

## What Are Custom Fields?

Custom Fields is a concept in IFS Cloud that lets you extend an existing logical unit in the application with additional, tailored information. These fields can then be surfaced on the pages that use that logical unit.

In plain terms: without touching the core application code, you can add your own fields to existing IFS screens — capturing data that's specific to your business, your industry, or your workflows.

The fields come in two flavors — persisted values, where users can enter and modify data directly, and read-only values that display existing data or calculated results.

## Why Does This Matter?

1. **Capture What Matters to Your Business**  
   Standard ERP fields are built for the broadest possible audience. Your business may need to track things that simply don't exist in the default data model — asset classifications, compliance flags, regional codes, internal scoring metrics. Custom Fields let you capture that data inside IFS, rather than in a sprawling spreadsheet sitting outside the system.

2. **No Code. No Risk.**  
   One of the most powerful aspects of Custom Fields in IFS Cloud is that they're configuration, not customization in the traditional sense. You can add custom fields to existing data models to capture additional information pertinent to specific business requirements — all without modifying the underlying application. This keeps your system upgrade-safe and your IT team sane.

3. **Data Lives Where It Belongs**  
   When business-critical information is scattered across emails, spreadsheets, and sticky notes, decisions suffer. Custom Fields bring that data into IFS — searchable, reportable, and tied to the right records. That means better visibility, cleaner audits, and more confident decision-making.

4. **Empower Business Users**  
   Custom Fields don't require a developer to configure. Business analysts and functional consultants can define and deploy them through the IFS administration interface. This shortens the gap between "we need this field" and "it's live in production."

## Real-World Use Cases

- **Manufacturing:** Track a custom quality grade or batch attribute on a work order
- **Field Service:** Add a site-specific risk classification to a service order
- **Asset Management:** Store a regulatory compliance date against a piece of equipment
- **Procurement:** Capture a preferred supplier tier or contract reference on a supplier record

## The Bottom Line

IFS Cloud is already powerful out of the box. But no ERP can anticipate every business nuance. Custom Fields give you the flexibility to tailor the data model to your unique operational requirements — without the cost, complexity, or risk of traditional customizations.

If you're not yet using Custom Fields in your IFS Cloud environment, you're likely storing important data somewhere it doesn't belong. It's time to bring it home.
